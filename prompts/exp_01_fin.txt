Objective:
Design and evaluate an equity trading strategy based on automatically discovered alpha factors
using daily OHLCV data.

Universe:
- US equities (liquid stocks, e.g. top 300 by average 60-day volume).
- Daily frequency.
- Long-short, dollar neutral portfolio.

Data:
- Use local OHLCV CSV files stored on disk
- Data location: ./data/ohlcv/
- One CSV file per asset
- Columns: date, open, high, low, close, volume
- Do NOT download data from the internet


Baseline:
- Equal-weighted long-short momentum strategy (20-day returns).
- Use this as a benchmark.

Factor discovery:
- Automatically generate candidate alpha factors based on:
  - momentum
  - mean reversion
  - volatility
  - volume
  - price ranges
- Factors must be explainable and simple (avoid overly complex expressions).

Backtesting protocol:
- Train: 2016–2020
- Validation: 2021–2022
- Test: 2023–2024
- Monthly rebalancing.
- Transaction costs: 10 bps per trade.
- Penalize monthly turnover above 40%.

Evaluation metrics:
- Annualized return
- Annualized volatility
- Sharpe ratio
- Max drawdown
- Monthly turnover
- Stability of performance across periods

Optimization goal:
- Improve Sharpe ratio on the test set relative to the baseline
- While keeping max drawdown and turnover under control

Output requirements:
1. Description of the top 3 discovered factors and their financial intuition
2. Backtest performance table vs baseline
3. Equity curves for train/validation/test
4. Discussion of robustness and risks
5. Final recommended strategy and rationale

Constraints:
- Prefer robustness over extreme performance.
- Do not overfit to the test period.
- If no factor clearly beats the baseline, explain why.
